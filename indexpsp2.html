Los Angeles International Airport (LAX)

Los Angeles International Airport, also known as LAX, holds the title of the busiest airport on the West Coast of the United States. This aviation hub serves as an international gateway, offering non-stop flights to more than 85 domestic and 69 international destinations. Located in Westchester, a neighborhood in the city of Los Angeles, LAX is an incredible place for plane spotting due to its diverse range of aircraft and airlines.

Best Times for Plane Spotting

Plane spotting at LAX is a rewarding experience throughout the year. However, as with any type of photography, lighting is everything. The golden hours of early morning and late afternoon provide ideal lighting conditions for capturing stunning images of aircraft. Furthermore, the sheer volume of traffic at LAX means that these time windows are filled with a constant stream of diverse aircraft. Late night spotting can also be rewarding as LAX is a 24/7 operation, with heavy cargo operations and late-night long-haul departures.

Best Locations for Viewing

The iconic In-N-Out Burger on Sepulveda Boulevard, located just off the northern runways (24L/24R), is a well-known location for plane spotting. Here, you can grab a delicious meal while enjoying the spectacle of aircraft on their final approach, just above your head. For a more panoramic perspective, Imperial Hill on the southern side of the airport provides excellent views of all four runways and terminal areas. Clutter's Park is another great spot, offering fantastic views of departing and arriving aircraft, especially during the afternoon golden hour.

Types of Aircraft You Can Spot

As one of the major international hubs, LAX showcases a vast array of aircraft. You can spot short-haul workhorses like the Airbus A320 and Boeing 737 series, as well as long-haul giants such as the Airbus A380 and the Boeing 747. A wide range of airlines from all around the world operate at LAX, giving you the chance to see a diverse collection of aircraft liveries. From national carriers to low-cost airlines, cargo giants to boutique operators, LAX hosts them all.

Runway Information

LAX boasts four parallel runways, specifically designed to handle the large traffic volume. The northern runways, 24L/06R and 24R/06L, are primarily used for departures, while the southern runways, 25L/07R and 25R/07L, cater mainly to arrivals. These runways range from 8,925 feet to 12,923 feet in length, making them capable of accommodating everything from small private jets to large, wide-body airliners. In addition, each runway is equipped with advanced instrument landing systems for safe operations in poor visibility conditions.

Flight Radar 24 and LAX

Enhancing your plane spotting experience at LAX is possible with the real-time flight tracking provided by Flight Radar 24. This useful tool offers detailed information about each flight, such as its origin, destination, aircraft type, altitude, and speed. By using Flight Radar 24, you can identify incoming flights, preparing you for what's coming and allowing you to set up your camera in advance. Tracking departing flights also provides an exciting insight into where they are heading, adding an additional layer of depth to your spotting experience.

Long Beach Airport (LGB) Spotting Guide

Long Beach Airport (LGB) is a different beast compared to its big brother LAX. It's smaller, quieter, and provides a more intimate spotting experience. LGB, with its beautiful Streamline Moderne terminal and fewer crowds, offers a more relaxed plane spotting adventure.

The airport is the base for JetBlue's west coast operations, so expect to see a lot of A320s. You'll also spot FedEx and UPS cargo planes, private jets, and even vintage aircraft from the adjacent museum.

Best Times for Plane Spotting

As LGB operates under a strict noise ordinance, commercial flights only take off and land between 7:00 AM and 10:00 PM. These are your spotting hours. JetBlue flights are most active in the morning (7:00 - 10:00 AM) and evening (6:00 - 10:00 PM).

Best Locations for Viewing

One of the best spots is the airport's official viewing area on Wardlow Road, just west of the runway. It offers unobstructed views of the runway and taxiways. There's also a grassy area with benches and picnic tables, perfect for a family day out.

For a different perspective, head over to Skylinks Golf Course. It's located south of the airport, and from here, you'll get great views of planes on final approach.

Types of Aircraft You Can Spot

At LGB, JetBlue's Airbus A320s are the main draw. But don't let that fool you. LGB is also home to Gulfstream Aerospace, so expect to see a lot of Gulfstream business jets. FedEx and UPS also operate cargo flights, and there's a healthy amount of general aviation traffic as well. If you're lucky, you might even spot vintage aircraft from the nearby museum taking to the skies.

Using Flight Radar 24 at LGB

Flight Radar 24 is a useful tool for spotting at LGB. As there are fewer flights compared to LAX, the app can help you plan your visit better. You'll know exactly when the next JetBlue flight is arriving or if a FedEx cargo plane is about to take off. This way, you can make sure you're at the viewing area at the right time.

LGB is a gem of an airport that offers a unique and enjoyable plane spotting experience. So, next time you're in the area, why not pay it a visit? And remember, patience is key in plane spotting. Happy spotting!
